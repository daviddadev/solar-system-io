  • Oort Cloud

  • 30,000 au - 1 ly

  • The Oort Cloud is a spherical shell of millions of icy bodies which surrounds the solar system at vast distances and is thought to be the birth place of long-period comets.

  • Kuiper Belt

  • 30 - 100 au

  • There is also a disk-shaped region of small icy bodies, which ranges from about 30 to 100 AU from the Sun, called the Kuiper Belt. The small icy bodies in the Kuiper Blet are understood to be the source of short-period comets, with orbital periods of more than two hundred years.

  • Comets

  • 30 au - 1 ly

  • A comet is know to be a relatively small, solid body of frozen water, carbon dioxide, ammonia, and methane, along with dusty and rocky bits of materials mixed in. Based on calculation of their observed paths, comets are estimated to originate some 30 AU to a light-year away or more from the Sun. A comet nears the Sun grows brighter, with the tail always pointing away from the Sun.

Learn more

  • Asteroids

  • Between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter is a belt, or circular region of thousands of small rocky bodies called asteroids. This belt contains thousands of asteroids that range in size from 1 kilometer or less to over 600 miles. Asteroids inside of the belt are made of stony material, and those on the outside are darker with carbon minerals. Most of the asteroids in the asteroid belt are about halfway between the Sun and Jupiter.

  • Meteoroids

  • In space, the remainings of a comets and asteroids are called meteoroids. When a meteoroid encounters Earth moving through space, it accelerates toward the surface with a speed that depends on its direction of travel. It soon begins to heats from air compression, melting into a visible trail of light and smoke.

  • Meteor

  • The streak of light and smoke in the sky is called a meteor. The "falling star" or "shooting star" is a meteor. Most meteors burn up or evaporate completely within seconds after reaching about 60 miles because they are nothing more than a a speck of dust. If a meteor happens to land on earth, the meteor becomes a meteorite.
